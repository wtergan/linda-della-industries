## Design Context

### Users
Owner-led small and midsize businesses in Baltimore and the Maryland–DC–Virginia corridor, especially teams of roughly 3–50 people. They are AI-curious but short on time, skeptical of hype, and need one practical workflow to save time, respond faster, or stop losing leads.

### Brand Personality
Grounded, technically serious, local, optimistic, and generational. Three working words: **civic, engineered, enduring**. Linda Della Industries honors the founder’s grandmothers while building toward a century-scale technology company.

### Aesthetic Direction
A daylight, editorial interpretation of 1930s Art Deco and mid-century optimistic futurism: World’s Fair geometry, NASA-era industrial graphics, architectural drafting, and restrained oil-paint texture. Use warm parchment, ink navy, oxidized brass, signal vermilion, and restrained teal. Avoid cyberpunk, neon, glassmorphism, generic purple/blue AI gradients, fake dashboards, and borrowed enterprise prestige.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with a concrete business outcome and a fixed first engagement—not the century vision.
2. Make technical competence visible through structure, precision, and plain language rather than model-name theater.
3. Use one memorable geometric “future built locally” visual system instead of stock AI imagery.
4. Keep the founder and Baltimore present; trust is a local, human advantage.
5. Earn every claim. No fake clients, testimonials, savings, certifications, or guarantees.
6. Mobile conversion and accessibility are first-class; motion must respect reduced-motion settings.
