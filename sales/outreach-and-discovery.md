# Outreach and discovery scripts

Use these as scaffolds. Personalize the first sentence. Do not pretend to have reviewed systems you have not seen.

## Warm introduction request

> Hey [Name] — I’m launching a small Baltimore AI systems studio focused on one practical job: taking a repetitive office workflow and putting it on rails in seven days, with a person still approving the important steps. I’m looking for one founding client at a flat $1,000 rate. Do you know an owner or operations manager who spends too much time copying leads, sorting an inbox, preparing recurring documents, or chasing follow-ups? An introduction would help more than a general share.

## Direct owner message

**Subject:** one repeated workflow at [Company]

> Hi [Name] — I’m William, a Baltimore software and AI engineer. I noticed [specific public signal: quote form, appointment intake, recurring report, service-request flow]. I’m not reaching out to replace your team or sell an AI seminar.
>
> I run a seven-day sprint that maps one repeated process and installs one human-reviewed workflow around tools you already use. The founding-client rate is $1,000 flat.
>
> If [specific task] is still handled by hand, would a 15-minute workflow conversation be useful? If it is not a fit, I’ll say so.
>
> [site URL]

## Short LinkedIn/Instagram DM

> Baltimore AI/software engineer here. I help owner-led teams remove one repeated admin bottleneck in seven days—one tested workflow, human approval, flat $1,000 founding rate. I saw [specific signal] at [company]. Is [task] still manual? If yes, I can map what a small first version would look like in 15 minutes.

## Partner/referral message

> I’m building a Baltimore workflow-implementation practice for the gap after a website, IT setup, or operations review: one narrow AI-assisted workflow built and handed over in seven days. I’m looking for web designers, MSPs, bookkeepers, and consultants who see repetitive client work but do not want to build integrations themselves. I’ll reciprocate referrals and keep scope transparent. Worth a 15-minute partner call?

## In-person opener

> I’m not here to pitch you on AI. I’m learning how local owners handle repetitive office work. What is one thing your team has to copy, sort, or follow up on every day?

Then listen. Ask permission before describing the sprint.

## Follow-up after no response

> Hi [Name] — one follow-up, then I’ll close the loop. If [specific workflow] is repetitive, I can sketch a small human-reviewed version before we discuss a build. If it is already working well, no need to reply.

## Fifteen-minute discovery call

1. What triggers the workflow?
2. Who touches it now, and in what order?
3. Which tools hold the input and output?
4. How often does it happen?
5. Where does it wait, get copied, or produce mistakes?
6. What decision must stay with a person?
7. What does a successful output look like?
8. Can we test with non-sensitive examples?
9. Who approves the scope and payment?
10. If the scope fits, are you ready to reserve a kickoff with a $500 deposit?

Do not spend the call teaching AI terminology. Reflect the workflow back in plain English.

## Close

> Based on what you showed me, I would scope the sprint as: when [trigger] happens, the system [actions], then [person] reviews [decision], and the approved result goes to [destination]. The seven-day founding rate is $1,000: $500 to reserve kickoff and $500 at handoff. Platform costs, if any, stay in your account. I can send the one-page scope today. Should I?

## Disqualify clearly

> This is too broad/high-stakes for the seven-day sprint. I would rather not take your money for the wrong scope. The smaller safe starting point would be [narrower workflow], or I can refer you to a specialist.
