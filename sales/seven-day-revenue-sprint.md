# Seven-day revenue sprint

**Window:** Friday, July 17 through Thursday, July 23, 2026  
**Primary goal:** one signed $1,000 Workflow Sprint.  
**Cash target:** one $500 kickoff deposit at minimum; collect the full $1,000 only when the client prefers prepayment.  
**Activity target:** 50 targeted touches → 5 workflow conversations → 2 proposals → 1 close.

The website supports the sale. It does not create the sale by itself.

## Non-negotiable offer

**The 7-Day AI Workflow Sprint — $1,000 founding-client rate**

Map one repeated process, build one human-reviewed workflow in tools the client already uses, test it, document it, and hand it over. Fourteen days of fixes are included. No generic consulting deck, employment-replacement promise, or guaranteed ROI.

## Daily operating block

1. **45 minutes — prospecting:** add 5–10 qualified names.
2. **60 minutes — outreach:** personalized messages, not mass mail.
3. **30 minutes — follow-up:** reply fast and update the tracker.
4. **60 minutes — demo/proposal work:** only material needed to close the next deal.
5. **30 minutes — social rep:** one call, voice note, in-person question, or meetup conversation.

Do not spend the week redesigning the site, making a complex logo, building a SaaS product, or learning a new automation platform.

## Prospect filters

Prioritize businesses that visibly receive recurring inquiries, quote requests, forms, documents, schedules, reports, or follow-ups. Good starting categories:

- property managers and real-estate service operators;
- HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, restoration, and specialty contractors;
- local agencies and consultancies;
- associations and small nonprofits;
- B2B service firms with 3–50 staff.

Exclude high-stakes regulated workflows from the first sprint.

## Qualification threshold

A prospect is qualified only if:

- the repeated process happens at least weekly;
- one owner can approve the scope;
- the workflow has a clear start and finish;
- inputs and expected outputs can be demonstrated with non-sensitive examples;
- the first version can keep a person at the approval step;
- the buyer can pay a kickoff deposit.

## Definition of success

Best case: $1,000 paid and kickoff scheduled.  
Acceptable commercial success: $500 deposit on a signed $1,000 engagement.  
Evidence of momentum: five real conversations, two proposals, and dated follow-ups.  
Failure mode to avoid: a polished brand with zero asks made.
