# Baltimore/DMV AI services market and launch offer

**Researched:** 2026-07-16  
**Decision:** launch one fixed-scope implementation offer, not a general AI consultancy.

## Executive conclusion

The fastest credible path to revenue is a **done-for-you workflow implementation sprint** for owner-led businesses with roughly 3–50 staff. Sell the removal of one visible administrative bottleneck—not “AI transformation,” generic education, a custom platform, or autonomous agents.

**Launch offer:** one tested, human-reviewed workflow configured for launch in seven days for a **$1,000 founding-client rate**. The client keeps the workflow and documentation; required platform/API costs are paid directly by the client.

A signed $1,000 engagement within seven days is plausible but not guaranteed. The controllable target is 50 well-selected outreach attempts, five real workflow conversations, two scoped proposals, and one paid kickoff deposit. The site is sales support; outreach is the revenue engine.

## Demand signals

### 1. Small-business adoption is still shallow

The [NFIB 2025 Small Business and Technology survey](https://www.nfib.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/2025-NFIB-Technology-Survey-1.pdf) reported:

- 42% of surveyed small-business owners were not at all familiar with AI tools designed for business operations or their industry.
- 24% currently used AI technologies for business activity.
- Among users, communications and marketing were much more common than process automation: only 4% reported using or planning to use AI for process automation.
- 30% of AI users reported increased productivity; 98% reported no employment change.

This supports a human-in-the-loop productivity offer. It does **not** support broad promises of revenue growth, headcount replacement, or guaranteed ROI.

### 2. The measured “core production” adoption rate is lower still

The U.S. Census Bureau’s [Annual Business Survey analysis](https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2025/12/technology-use-and-business-conditions.html) reported that 8.8% of businesses used AI to produce goods or services in 2023, while 14.5% planned to use it. This is a narrower measure than casual use of tools such as ChatGPT. The difference reinforces the implementation gap: experimentation is more common than embedded workflow use.

### 3. Baltimore/DMV demand is visible in current programming

- The [Maryland Black Chamber of Commerce calendar](https://marylandbcc.org/calendar/) lists “Foundations for the AI-Enabled CEO” on July 23 and July 30, 2026, plus a July 30 networking mixer.
- Baltimore City’s 2026 Small Business Advancement Conference included a “Leveraging AI for Business” workshop.
- The [Maryland SBDC AI resources](https://marylandsbdc.org/ai-resources/) page offers advising and workshops.

The practical implication: local education already exists and is often free. Linda Della should not lead with generic AI literacy. The paid wedge is **mapping, building, testing, and handing over one workflow**.

## Chosen customer

Start with owner-led organizations in Baltimore and the DMV that:

- have 3–50 staff;
- use email, spreadsheets, forms, a calendar, and possibly a simple CRM;
- repeat the same information-handling task every day or week;
- can let one owner or operations lead approve a decision quickly;
- do not require sensitive medical, legal, credit, hiring, or regulated-data handling for the pilot.

**Best first lanes:** specialty contractors/home services, property-management operations, agencies/consultancies, associations and small nonprofits, local B2B service firms, and other administrative businesses.

**Avoid first:** healthcare records, legal advice, lending/credit, hiring decisions, autonomous cold outreach, government procurement, or a multi-system rebuild. These add risk, trust, or sales-cycle length that conflicts with a seven-day cash objective.

## Launch offer

### The 7-Day AI Workflow Sprint — $1,000 founding-client rate

**Included**

1. One 60-minute workflow mapping session.
2. One narrow automation built around tools the client already uses.
3. Human approval at consequential steps.
4. Test scenarios, failure-path notes, and a live or recorded handoff.
5. Fourteen days of fixes after launch.

**Good pilot examples**

- Web inquiry → structured intake → draft reply → human approval → CRM/sheet entry.
- Shared inbox → request classification → field extraction → routing queue.
- Meeting notes → decisions/tasks → owner review → follow-up draft.
- Repeated document → reusable table → exception queue for a human.

**Commercial guardrails**

- The founding rate is for one workflow with one decision-maker and a seven-day scope.
- Platform/API charges are paid directly by the client.
- No guaranteed savings, sales, or revenue figure.
- No unsupervised high-stakes decisions or mass outreach.
- Suggested payment terms: $500 to reserve kickoff and $500 at handoff; offer full prepayment only if the client prefers it.

## Immediate distribution channels

1. **Warm network first:** former coworkers, Morgan State alumni, friends/family who know business owners, and prior operations contacts. Ask for introductions, not purchases.
2. **Local Black-business ecosystem:** Maryland Black Chamber, Greater Baltimore Black Chamber, The Black Mall/Mind Your Business, Baltimore business-resource programs, and relevant local meetups.
3. **Targeted local outreach:** owner-led businesses with a visible repeated intake, inbox, reporting, or follow-up process. Send a short personalized observation, never a generic “your website is bad” pitch.
4. **Marketplace backup:** one tightly scoped Upwork project and 5–10 relevant proposals; do not race to the lowest price.
5. **Partner channel:** web designers, managed IT providers, bookkeepers, and consultants who see workflow pain but do not build AI integrations.

## Research cautions

- Vendor case studies and “average savings” claims are often selected marketing examples.
- National adoption surveys do not prove Baltimore-specific willingness to pay.
- Event listings demonstrate interest and access to prospects, not demand for this exact offer.
- The offer must be validated through actual conversations and deposits.
